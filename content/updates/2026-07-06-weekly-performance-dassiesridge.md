---
title: "Weekly performance: Dassiesridge (29 Jun - 5 Jul)"
date: "2026-07-06"
---

Dassiesridge Hybrid (Wind + BESS) closed the week net positive, exporting above its declared ALP in 61% of periods for a net gain of 1,399.7 MWh (2,997.3 MWh gained against 1,597.6 MWh lost during shortfall periods). Declared utilisation held at 98.5%, with actual exports tracking 97.7% of declared capacity.

Wind speeds were low all week, averaging around 3 m/s, yet wind still supplied the bulk of exported energy while BESS contributed roughly 15%. The battery cycled hard to smooth the shortfalls: 659.7 MWh charged against 582.2 MWh discharged, with average SOC sitting at 38.8%. When the site did dip into a net loss period, it took an average of 10.7 hours to recover to net-positive operation.

All 14 wind turbines and the battery fleet remained operationally available with no faults this week. One active critical alarm is outstanding as of writing: a combustible gas detection alert on the battery container, raised today (6 Jul) and not yet acknowledged.

Data note: this week's BESS State-of-Health readings in the production model logged 0% across all 145 hourly records, conflicting with the portal's live reading of 98.2%. This looks like a data pipeline gap rather than an actual battery health issue, but is worth a check.
